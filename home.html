Computer Vision based Wheelchair Navigation System

For blind wheelchair users, safe navigation is not just a matter of convenience but a fundamental requirement for independence and well-being. Everyday environments— whether indoors with narrow pathways or outdoors with sidewalks, intersections, and traffic— can be risky when visual cues are unavailable.

Audio

The Challenge

Traditional wheelchairs, even powered ones, cannot “see” their surroundings. Without environmental awareness, users face hazards such as curbs, drop-offs, or unexpected obstacles. These risks limit independence and mobility, making everyday travel stressful and unsafe.

The Solution: Computer Vision

Computer vision allows a computer to “see” through cameras and process what it observes. By attaching cameras to a wheelchair and applying smart algorithms, the system can recognize obstacles, detect changes in terrain, and warn the user instantly.

This makes the wheelchair “smarter,” giving it the ability to support safe and confident travel for blind users.

What the System Should Do

  • Detect and classify obstacles, including curbs and drop-offs.
  • Recognize environmental contexts such as sidewalks, intersections, and roadways.
  • Provide real-time feedback through speech, audio tones, or tactile signals.

By combining these features, a vision-based wheelchair navigation system could transform mobility—turning unsafe routes into accessible journeys. Navigation feedback can be given as speech, tones, or vibrations for accessibility.
