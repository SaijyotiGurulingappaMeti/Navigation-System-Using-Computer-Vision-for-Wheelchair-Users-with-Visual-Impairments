Challenges

Navigating safely is a major concern for blind wheelchair users. In this section, we explore the technical, environmental, and user-related challenges faced by such systems, followed by possible directions for future improvements to enhance safety, reliability, and user experience.

Audio

Technical Challenges

  • Stereo vision depth maps are noisy, especially for small curbs at a distance.
  • Algorithms for terrain detection and path planning require high computation power.
  • Affordable hardware has limited processing capacity for real-time vision.

Environmental Challenges

  • Strong sunlight, shadows, or nighttime reduce camera accuracy.
  • Reflections from wet pavement or glass can cause misclassification.
  • Moving obstacles like cars, bicycles, and pedestrians are harder to detect and predict.

User-Related Challenges

  • Too many alerts (audio or tactile) can overwhelm the user.
  • Feedback must be minimal, clear, and actionable (e.g., “turn left” instead of continuous beeps).
  • Systems must balance autonomy with user control so that users feel safe and independent.

Future Work

  • Increase robustness so the wheelchair can handle all lighting conditions, including bright sunlight, shadows, and nighttime. This may be achieved by combining regular cameras with infrared or depth cameras.
  • Improve dynamic obstacle handling—future systems should detect static curbs and objects and also track and predict the movement of pedestrians, bicycles, or vehicles in real time.
  • Use advances in embedded AI hardware to run complex vision algorithms on low-cost processors, making the system faster and more practical for everyday use.
  • Enhance user interaction by providing multimodal feedback (speech, tones, vibrations) that adapts to the user’s preference, ensuring guidance is clear but not overwhelming.
  • Research adaptive autonomy, allowing the system to balance giving full control to the user and automatically intervening to prevent accidents, enabling blind users to feel both safe and independent.
