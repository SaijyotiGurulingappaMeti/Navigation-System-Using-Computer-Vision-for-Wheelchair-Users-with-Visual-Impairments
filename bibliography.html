Bibliography

  • M. Coughlan and H. Shen, “Terrain analysis for blind wheelchair users: Computer vision algorithms for finding curbs and other negative obstacles.” in CVHI, 2007.
    Presents early computer vision methods to detect curbs and negative obstacles for blind wheelchair users. Useful but dated, tested on limited conditions.
    Reliability: Moderate — older work with limited validation.
  • B. Li et al., "Vision-Based Mobile Indoor Assistive Navigation Aid for Blind People," in IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, vol. 18, no. 3, pp. 702-714, 1 March 2019, doi: 10.1109/TMC.2018.2842751.
    Proposes a vision-based indoor navigation system with SLAM and obstacle detection, validated in complex environments.
    Reliability: High — peer-reviewed in a top IEEE journal, strong validation.
  • M. Z. Hasan, S. Sikder and M. A. Rahaman, "Real-Time Computer Vision Based Autonomous Navigation System for Assisting Visually Impaired People using Machine Learning," 2022 4th International Conference on Sustainable Technologies for Industry 4.0 (STI), Dhaka, Bangladesh, 2022, pp. 1-6, doi: 10.1109/STI56238.2022.10103268.
    Introduces a real-time ML-based system for obstacle detection and path planning to assist visually impaired users.
    Reliability: Moderate — innovative but validated on limited datasets.
  • Kim, Eun. (2016). Wheelchair Navigation System for disabled and Elderly People. Sensors. 16. 1806. 10.3390/s16111806.
    Presents a smart wheelchair using sensors and vision for safe navigation, aimed at elderly and disabled users.
    Reliability: High — published in a strong journal with practical experiments.
  • Yu, H., & Saniie, J. (2025). Visual Impairment Spatial Awareness (VISA) Method for Indoor Navigation. Nature Machine Intelligence. Link
    Introduces VISA, an AI-based spatial awareness method for real-time indoor navigation of visually impaired users.
    Reliability: Very High — recent and published in a leading AI journal.
  • ResearchGate. (2025). Smart Wheelchair Controlled Through a Vision-Based Autonomous System. Link
    Explores vision-based smart wheelchair control for autonomous navigation and user independence.
    Reliability: Low — preprint without peer review, not formally validated.
  • Slavin, T. et al. (2022). Vision-Based Low-Cost Power Wheelchair Assistive Driving System for Smartphones. CSPECC, UTSA. Link
    Proposes a smartphone-based affordable wheelchair assistive driving system using vision algorithms.
    Reliability: Moderate to Low — smaller venue, exploratory validation.
  • PathFinder Team. (2025). Map-less Navigation Using Monocular Depth Estimation and Vision-Language Models. arXiv. Link
    Develops a flexible map-less navigation approach combining depth estimation and vision-language reasoning.
    Reliability: Moderate — cutting-edge but only a preprint, not yet peer-reviewed.
  • ScienceDirect. (2024). A Comprehensive Review of Navigation Systems for Visually Impaired Individuals. Link
    Provides a broad review of navigation systems for visually impaired users, covering sensors, vision, and AI approaches.
    Reliability: High — peer-reviewed review, strong for background context.
  • ACM Digital Library. (2025). A Survey on Assistive Technologies for Visually Impaired Individuals. Link
    Survey of assistive technologies such as wearables, smart wheelchairs, and AI navigation aids.
    Reliability: Very High — ACM peer-reviewed, comprehensive and up-to-date.
