Implementation / Expected Results

In this section, we will explore how the previously discussed algorithms can be applied in real wheelchair navigation systems. It also highlights the expected results and benefits each algorithm brings to improving safety and independence for blind users.

Audio

Stereo Vision & Disparity Maps

Implementation: Dual cameras capture left and right views. A disparity map is computed to estimate depth. The wheelchair uses this to detect curbs, drop-offs, and obstacles in real-time.

Expected Results: Accurate depth measurements within 0.5–5 meters. Users receive timely warnings before obstacles, improving safety indoors and outdoors.

Texture & Shape Filtering (HOG Features)

Implementation: Image blocks are analyzed for textures and edges using HOG. Surfaces like sidewalks, grass, or roads are classified. Shape analysis identifies intersections and pathways.

Expected Results: Classifies safe paths vs obstacles with ~90% accuracy in controlled environments. Improves route planning and prevents navigation errors.

Background Subtraction & Occupancy Grids

Implementation: Moving objects (pedestrians, vehicles) are detected using background subtraction. Occupancy grids map safe and risky zones. Machine learning classifiers decide the wheelchair's next action.

Expected Results: Real-time detection of dynamic obstacles. Enhanced safety and collision avoidance in crowded or changing environments.
